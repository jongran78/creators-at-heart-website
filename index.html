about

Carol has been an accomplished art teacher for over 30 years and since retiring has delved into pleinair painting as her primary interest. Charlie was a successfully talented graphic designer who has taken his design background and applied it directly to sculpture and jewelry. Both Carol and Charlie are likewise talented Photographers. They spent over 8 years developing a successful photography business yet, as all things change....Years later, Carol and Charlie are entering a new passage in life.

A time to create.

And to think it all started with a personal ad....Sculptor seeks model...

portfolio

Both Carol and Charlie fell in love with the camera and the still image circa 2007. They dove in head first, absorbing all they could about lighting, portrature, and studio techniques-even building a collapsable make-shift studio in their garage. They developed their own process for photographing weddings as well as landscapes. While they have since retired the business aspect of photography, they still enjoy framing visually stimulating images that breath life into any room.
After partaking in a 3 month seminar and workshop on jewelry making, both Carol and Charlie have set about sculpting unique wearable art- Mostly made of plyable special clays. With a number of various designs, these pieces are meant to compliment your style, whichever it may be. Each piece comes in your choice of color or material.

Carol has been an avid plein air painter with an appetite for travel and cultural diversity. She has painted in a variety of locals from Ireland to Taiwan and Uganda. Though she paints with an array of paint mediums, her go-to is acrylic due to her quest for brilliant colors and the textural properties of acrylic paints. "I am ever intrugued by the beauty of earths geography, remote locations and the people who inhabit them." "I'm curious about how the local people adapt to their location and what technology exists because of their relation to the land."

Inspired by the natural kettles and moraines of the country side where he grew up, Charlie sculpts fluid, abstract objects that could fill a public space just as eloquently as a display shelf of a modern home. Recently he has been taken by the onset of the 3-D printer and has employed them to create his most recent works.
